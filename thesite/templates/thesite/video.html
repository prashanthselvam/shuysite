Senior Thesis

The following video is a documentation of my Senior Thesis video installation at Connecticut College. Inspired by the discrepancies in memory, I aim to bring attention to the human tendency to construct distorted recollections of the past. Although the inability to fully remember every detail of our lives can be perceived as a weakness, I instead see it as one of our greatest strengths: this inability provides space to exercise creative thought. When we bridge the gaps in our memory with fictions we do so using our knowledge of the world around us, building an imaginative perception of events past. We imagine and invent when memory fails.

Compositing and animation
Adobe Premiere:
Video and sound editing

Memory Lapse

This is the full video of Memory Lapse from my Senior Thesis Exhibition at Connecticut College. The overlap and conflict that emerges from individuals’ differing recollections of the same event, is what I call comparative memory discrepancy. Levels of certainty and self-doubt come into question when a memory is challenged by an opposing interpretation. This newly informed realization at times could create a surprising distortion of what is believed to be true.
After Effects:
Compositing and animation
Adobe Premiere:
Video and sound editing
Filmed in Toronto, Canada
