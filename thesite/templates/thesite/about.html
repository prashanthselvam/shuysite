A little about me

Finding inspiration from my favorite video game characters, I took an interest in drawing at a very young age. I began honing my talents in the visual arts by attending Cardinal Carter Academy for the Arts in Toronto. I then continued to study studio art as well as art history at Connecticut College, learning and mastering a variety of mediums. In the fall of 2012 I studied abroad in Florence, Italy and was introduced to Video Art and 3-D Animation with the use of Adobe Photoshop, Premiere, After Effects and Autodesk Maya. In the summer of 2013 I interned at Youth Digital Studio in Durham, North Carolina where I assisted in teaching students how to create 3-D computer games. From initial concept sketch to polished interactive game, the experience trained me to become proficient in new software, teach this knowledge to the students in an engaging way, and strive to solve any technical problems that arose. Through my College's in-depth visual arts program I have developed skills in digital art, graphic design and animation, and aspire to build on them with a career in the film, game or advertising industry.
